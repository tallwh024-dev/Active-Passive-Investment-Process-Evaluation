# Project Summary

This project evaluates a firm's investment process using 10 years of monthly return data from January 2016 through December 2025.

## Objective

The Chief Investment Officer wants to understand which parts of the investment process are adding value and which parts are weaker. The project evaluates both allocation decisions and active fund implementation.

## Main evaluation areas

The analysis covers multi-asset allocation, capitalization, country selection, duration, credit quality, alternatives allocation, and active fund implementation.

## Dimension-level results

| Dimension | Excess Return | Tracking Error | Information Ratio |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Capitalization | 0.000512 | 0.000375 | 1.367 |
| Country | 0.000811 | 0.001056 | 0.767 |
| Duration | 0.001002 | 0.001836 | 0.546 |
| Credit | 0.000233 | 0.001145 | 0.204 |
| Alternatives | 0.000307 | 0.002796 | 0.110 |
| Multi-Asset 60/30/10 | 0.000752 | 0.001820 | 0.413 |
| Multi-Asset 50/45/5 | 0.000722 | 0.001384 | 0.522 |

## Interpretation

The strongest dimension-level results are Capitalization, Country, and Duration. Credit and Alternatives were positive but weaker.

The strongest active fund results came from IWB, IXUS, and TLT. Weaker results came from IWM, SHV, IGSB, and IGF.

## Recommendation

The firm should retain active emphasis in areas that demonstrated consistent value added and review active implementation in areas with weak or negative information ratios.

## Conclusion

The investment process generated positive value overall, but results were uneven across asset classes. A practical improvement would be to keep active management where information ratios are strong and increase passive implementation where active results have been weak.
